Now

I’ve reformulated my current focus, as of August 22, 2020, in term of OKRs. I have 4 OKRs, two related and two unrelated to work. All my OKRs are time-framed to the third quarter of 2020 (end date: September 30, 2020).

Work-related OKRs

O1. Make sense of data on errors and find ways to prevent them

KR1. Lead team initiative during cooldown period to triage all Sentry errors. Assess the team’s health based on result.
KR2. Automate some steps in software production
KR3. Gather collective ideas on best practices

O2. Grasp Elixir

KR1. Read Elixir in Action, Real-Time Phoenix and make presentation
KR2. Write plug with a FOSS license

Personal OKRs

O3. Improve Estonian

KR1. Finish Keeleklikk A1
KR2. Study 3 hours per week
KR3. Review Anki cards daily

O4. Improve running stats

KR1. Run a total of 200km
KR2. Run a 1-km lap in 4’30”

I keep score of my past areas of focus in the retrospectives page.

To the imaginary friend that I haven’t seen in a while, asking me how it’s going:

I’ve been working in a new company, almost for 4 months now, and it’s (still) great.

My life-long journey to becoming a good engineer is going on. I’m currently trying to find projects that I can release as FOSS. With that in place, I’d like to build one talk around them to present in some conference in the future. There’s a Meetup that one of my colleagues organizes in Tartu, which can be a good first stage to collect and test ideas.

I’m still running. I’m now going to bars and restaurants. I don’t plan to visit Italy soon.

I’m slowly abandoning LibraryThing. I love their work but their site is just too slow to be usable.
